📐 The Formula Explained

School Quality Score (SQS) — Secondary

SQS = (A8_norm × 0.35) + (EM_norm × 0.30) + (EBaccAPS_norm × 0.20) + (EBaccEnt_norm × 0.15) where each _norm = (School value / England average) × 100 England averages used: A8 = 46.1 | Eng&Maths% = 45.4 | EBaccAPS = 4.09 | EBaccEntry% = 40.5
35%
Attainment 8
Average score across 8 GCSEs — the broadest headline measure of overall academic achievement
30%
Grade 5+ Eng & Maths
Strong pass rate in the two most critical subjects — most useful real-world outcome for pupils
20%
EBacc APS
Average point score across 5 EBacc pillars — reflects academic depth and curriculum quality
15%
EBacc Entry %
Proportion of pupils entered for the full EBacc — signals the school's academic ambition

Primary Quality Score (PQS) — Primary

PQS = (RWM_norm × 0.45) + (RWMhigh_norm × 0.15) + (Reading_norm × 0.20) + (Maths_norm × 0.20) where each _norm = (School value / England average) × 100 England (KS2 2025) averages: RWM expected% = 61 | RWM higher% = 8 | Reading SS = 105.0 | Maths SS = 104.5
45%
RWM Expected Standard
% reaching the expected standard in reading, writing & maths combined — the headline KS2 measure
15%
RWM Higher Standard
% working at greater depth across all three — rewards stretch for the most able pupils
20%
Reading Scaled Score
Average KS2 reading scaled score — the foundation literacy measure
20%
Maths Scaled Score
Average KS2 maths scaled score — the foundation numeracy measure

Why normalise?

Each metric is divided by its England national average and multiplied by 100. This puts all four metrics on the same scale before weighting, so a school scoring 100 on any metric is exactly at the national average, and scores above 100 are above average.

180 + Exceptional
160–179 Outstanding
140–159 Well above avg
120–139 Above average
< 120 Near / below avg

🔢 Worked Example — AGSB

Altrincham Grammar School for Boys (2025)

Step 1 — Normalise each metric: A8 = 78.5 / 46.1 × 100 = 170.3 Eng&M = 98.5 / 45.4 × 100 = 216.9 APS = 7.21 / 4.09 × 100 = 176.3 Entry = 53.0 / 40.5 × 100 = 130.9 Step 2 — Apply weights: (170.3 × 0.35) + (216.9 × 0.30) + (176.3 × 0.20) + (130.9 × 0.15) = 59.6 + 65.1 + 35.3 + 19.6 = SQS 179.6

An SQS of 179.6 means AGSB’s weighted performance is 79.6% above the England national average.

🔢 Worked Example — Navigation Primary

Navigation Primary School, Altrincham (2025)

Step 1 — Normalise each metric: RWM = 92 / 61 × 100 = 150.8 Higher = 30 / 8 × 100 = 375.0 Read = 112 / 105.0 × 100 = 106.7 Maths = 110 / 104.5 × 100 = 105.3 Step 2 — Apply weights: (150.8 × 0.45) + (375.0 × 0.15) + (106.7 × 0.20) + (105.3 × 0.20) = 67.9 + 56.2 + 21.3 + 21.1 = PQS 166.5

A PQS of 166.5 means Navigation Primary’s weighted KS2 performance is 66.5% above the England national average.

📌 Data Notes & Caveats
  • Use the Secondary / Primary toggle to switch phase. Secondary schools are ranked by SQS from 2024/25 GCSE data; primary schools by PQS from 2024/25 KS2 SATs data. The region tabs and type checkboxes apply to whichever phase is active.
  • Secondary (SQS) uses Attainment 8, Grade 5+ English & Maths, EBacc APS and EBacc Entry. Primary (PQS) uses % reaching the expected and higher standards in reading, writing & maths combined, plus average reading and maths scaled scores.
  • Progress measures are unavailable for 2025/26 at both key stages — COVID-19 disrupted the 2020/21 baseline assessments — so both formulas use attainment only. They are expected to return in 2027.
  • Many figures (especially EBacc entry, and all independent / prep school values) are approximate / illustrative where exact 2025 published data was not available; independents largely sit IGCSEs/internal assessments outside DfE reporting. Treat rankings as indicative.
  • The England benchmark row shows 100 for all normalised metrics by definition and appears at the foot of every table.
  • Each region lists up to the top ~90 state-funded schools by attainment (≈190 secondary and ≈190 primary across both regions), plus a selection of independent / prep schools so the type filter has meaningful results. The table scrolls horizontally on narrow screens.
