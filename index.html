📐 The Formula Explained

School Quality Score (SQS)

SQS = (A8_norm × 0.35) + (EM_norm × 0.30) + (EBaccAPS_norm × 0.20) + (EBaccEnt_norm × 0.15) where each _norm = (School value / England average) × 100 England averages used: A8 = 46.1 | Eng&Maths% = 45.4 | EBaccAPS = 4.09 | EBaccEntry% = 40.5
35%
Attainment 8
Average score across 8 GCSEs — the broadest headline measure of overall academic achievement
30%
Grade 5+ Eng & Maths
Strong pass rate in the two most critical subjects — most useful real-world outcome for pupils
20%
EBacc APS
Average point score across 5 EBacc pillars — reflects academic depth and curriculum quality
15%
EBacc Entry %
Proportion of pupils entered for the full EBacc — signals the school's academic ambition

Why normalise?

Each metric is divided by its England national average and multiplied by 100. This puts all four metrics on the same scale before weighting, so a school scoring 100 on any metric is exactly at the national average, and scores above 100 are above average.

🔢 Worked Example — AGSB

Altrincham Grammar School for Boys (2025)

Step 1 — Normalise each metric: A8 = 78.5 / 46.1 × 100 = 170.3 Eng&M = 98.5 / 45.4 × 100 = 216.9 APS = 7.21 / 4.09 × 100 = 176.3 Entry = 53.0 / 40.5 × 100 = 130.9 Step 2 — Apply weights: (170.3 × 0.35) + (216.9 × 0.30) + (176.3 × 0.20) + (130.9 × 0.15) = 59.6 + 65.1 + 35.3 + 19.6 = SQS 179.6

An SQS of 179.6 means AGSB’s weighted performance is 79.6% above the England national average.

📌 Data Notes & Caveats
  • All figures are from the 2024/25 GCSE academic year (published February 2026), sourced from DfE, Locrating, OwlTutors, and individual school websites.
  • Progress 8 is unavailable for 2025 and 2026 — COVID-19 prevented KS2 assessments in 2020/21, making it impossible to calculate a baseline. It will return in 2027.
  • Use the region tabs to switch between Greater Manchester and Greater London, and the type dropdown to filter by grammar, state (non-selective) or private / independent schools.
  • EBacc Entry % and EBacc APS for many schools are approximate / illustrative where exact 2025 published figures were not yet available; treat cross-borough rankings as indicative.
  • Independent schools typically enter IGCSEs that fall outside DfE Attainment 8 / EBacc reporting, so their figures here are indicative estimates for comparison only.
  • AGGS EBacc APS is estimated from the school's published 92.7% EBacc pass rate at grade 5+.
  • The England benchmark row shows 100 for all normalised metrics by definition and appears at the foot of each regional table.
  • Tables now include a selection of state grammar, state non-selective and independent schools so the type filter has meaningful results in each region.
