📐 The Formula Explained

School Quality Score (SQS) — Secondary

SQS = (A8_norm × 0.35) + (EM_norm × 0.30) + (EBaccAPS_norm × 0.20) + (EBaccEnt_norm × 0.15) where each _norm = (School value / England average) × 100 England averages used: A8 = 46.1 | Eng&Maths% = 45.4 | EBaccAPS = 4.09 | EBaccEntry% = 40.5

Why normalise? (Secondary SQS)

Each metric is divided by its England national average and multiplied by 100. This puts all four metrics on the same scale before weighting, so a school scoring 100 on any metric is exactly at the national average, and scores above 100 are above average.

Reading the primary indices

Primary indices are scored on a 0–100 scale. For API and Grammar Readiness, each input is a national percentile — a score of 75 means the school is ahead of 75% of all England primaries on that measure. The England average works out at ≈49. (11+ Readiness instead ranks each school 0–100 within its own local authority.)

🔢 Worked Example — AGSB

Altrincham Grammar School for Boys (2025)

Step 1 — Normalise each metric: A8 = 78.5 / 46.1 × 100 = 170.3 Eng&M = 98.5 / 45.4 × 100 = 216.9 APS = 7.21 / 4.09 × 100 = 176.3 Entry = 53.0 / 40.5 × 100 = 130.9 Step 2 — Apply weights: (170.3 × 0.35) + (216.9 × 0.30) + (176.3 × 0.20) + (130.9 × 0.15) = 59.6 + 65.1 + 35.3 + 19.6 = SQS 179.6

An SQS of 179.6 means AGSB’s weighted performance is 79.6% above the England national average.

🔢 Worked Example — Navigation Primary

Navigation Primary School, Altrincham (2025)

Raw 2025 KS2 values: RWM expected 72% | RWM higher 13% | Reading SS 108 | Maths SS 108 Step 1 — Convert each to a national percentile: Expected = 71.1 (ahead of 71% of England primaries) Higher = 78.0 Reading = 80.4 Maths = 87.5 Step 2 — Apply Academic Performance Index weights: (71.1 × 0.40) + (78.0 × 0.40) + (80.4 × 0.10) + (87.5 × 0.10) = 28.4 + 31.2 + 8.0 + 8.8 = API 76.4

An API of 76.4 places Navigation Primary in roughly the top quarter of primary schools nationally, on a 0–100 scale where the England average sits at 48.8.

📌 Data Notes & Caveats
  • Use the Secondary / Primary toggle to switch phase. Secondary schools are ranked by SQS from 2024/25 GCSE data; primary schools by one of three percentile indices from 2024/25 KS2 SATs data, chosen from the "Rank by" selector. The region tabs and type checkboxes apply to whichever phase is active.
  • Secondary (SQS) uses Attainment 8, Grade 5+ English & Maths, EBacc APS and EBacc Entry, each normalised against the England average (100 = average). Primary offers the Academic Performance Index (40% expected / 40% higher / 10% reading / 10% maths), Grammar School Readiness (50% higher / 25% reading / 25% maths) and 11+ Readiness (the same blend normalised within each local authority). Each input is a national percentile (0–100) computed against all 15,233 England KS2 schools, so the indices are not double-counting the reading/maths signal on different scales.
  • Progress measures are unavailable for 2025/26 at both key stages — COVID-19 disrupted the 2020/21 baseline assessments — so both formulas use attainment only. They are expected to return in 2027.
  • Many figures (especially EBacc entry, and all independent / prep school values) are approximate / illustrative where exact 2025 published data was not available; independents largely sit IGCSEs/internal assessments outside DfE reporting. Treat rankings as indicative.
  • The England benchmark row shows 100 for all normalised metrics by definition and appears at the foot of every table.
  • Each region lists up to the top ~90 state-funded schools by attainment (≈190 secondary and ≈190 primary across both regions), plus a selection of independent / prep schools so the type filter has meaningful results. The table scrolls horizontally on narrow screens.
